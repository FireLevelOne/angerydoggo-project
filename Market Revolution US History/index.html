Market Revolution

A revolution where people were switching from making everything by hand and living off the land into working for money and exchanging that for things they cant make.





Free Enterprise

An enviroment where organizations compete with one another free of state control.





Entrepreneurs

People who take the ultimate risk of quiting whatever line of repetitive work or daily rutine in search of building a buisness or producing a successful product or service.





Samuel F. B. Morse

Inventer and innovator of the Telegraph





Lowell Textile Mills

Mills where cloth was mass produced and 3/4 of the workers were female.





Strike

An organized refusal to work until certain demands are met.





Immigration

The beginning of the Market Revolution brought many people to the United States for work.





National Trades Union

The first organization to care about wage earner rights





The Commonwealth v Hunt

A supreme court case that legalized labor combinations as long as they used legal means to acheive their goals.
